02 — Executive Summary

What We Are Building

DealatCity is becoming a consignment marketplace on Odoo V19. Vendors connect their own systems via API and push products and stock directly into a central Odoo catalog. They get a personal portal page that shows their on-consignment stock, what has sold, and the value of those sales — so they can raise an invoice to DealatCity for sold items only. Both parties share the same source of truth.

Delivered in 12 weeks, milestone-based, by a Kuwait-based team with hands-on Odoo experience. Knowledge transfer is built in so DealatCity owns the platform, not just the deliverable.

  • Vendor API & PortalLogin, API push of products & stock, vendor visibility page.
  • Consignment SalesSold-item tracking, vendor invoice workflow, settlements.
  • Inventory & WarehouseSKU, barcode, variants, valuation, multi-location.
  • Full Accounting CycleAP/AR, GL, assets, financial reporting.
  • Website & API SyncReal-time orders, products, customers.
End-to-End Flow: Vendor pushes products & stock via API → stock lands in Odoo as consignment → admin approves & publishes to dealatcity.com → customer orders → both vendor and DealatCity see what was sold and at what value → vendor raises an invoice for sold items → DealatCity records the bill and settles per agreed terms.

PILLAR 5.4

Consignment Sales, Vendor Visibility & Invoicing

Sale-or-return / consignment model. Stock stays on the vendor's books until DealatCity sells it. Vendor invoices DealatCity only for what was sold.

Vendor Visibility Page (Odoo Portal)

  • Live view of vendor's products listed in DealatCity
  • Current consignment stock by SKU and location
  • Sold-items report: date, qty, customer (anonymised), value
  • Outstanding amount available to invoice
  • Returns & cancellations affecting consignment stock
  • Filter by date, product, store, status
  • Export to PDF / Excel for vendor's own records

Consignment Accounting & Invoicing

  • Stock recorded in consignment location — vendor retains ownership
  • No vendor bill on stock receipt (consignment ≠ purchase)
  • On customer sale: COGS posted at agreed consignment cost
  • Sold-item ledger updated for both vendor and DealatCity
  • Vendor raises invoice from portal (or uploads their own) for sold items
  • DealatCity admin validates against actual sales & records vendor bill
  • Settlement per agreed cycle (weekly / monthly) with payment-run export
  • Dispute / reconciliation workflow for mismatched lines
Consignment Stock Sold-Item Ledger Vendor Invoice Vendor Bill Settlement
DeliverableConsignment accounting live, vendor portal page showing stock & sold items, vendor-raised invoice workflow operational.
Why consignment matters: DealatCity carries no inventory risk — vendors retain ownership until a customer buys. The vendor only invoices for what actually sells, so cashflow stays clean for both sides. The shared visibility (same data, both parties) eliminates "what did I sell, how much do you owe me" disputes that kill marketplace relationships.

12. Notices & Communication

13. Assignment

Neither party may assign this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other, save that the Developer may sub-contract specialised tasks while remaining responsible for delivery.

14. Severability & Entire Agreement

If any clause is held unenforceable, the remaining clauses survive. This Agreement, together with any executed Change Requests, constitutes the entire understanding between the parties and supersedes all prior discussions.
